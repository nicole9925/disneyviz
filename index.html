Center of Applied Internet Analysis

La Jolla, CA / 2020 - Curr / Web Application Development Intern REU

Following the completion of my first data visualization class, I realized that I enjoyed studying and practicing the intersection of computer science and data science - analyzing data, then creating a visual experience to communicate it. Thus, I decided that the best way to do that would be to begin creating web applications. This job, my first official developer position, opened my eyes to the process of developing actual application deliverables to be used for the public, from the basics of working coorperatively remotely to extensive detail-oriented research to efficient programming.

Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute

La Jolla, CA / 2019 / Data Science Instructional Assistant

I was an instructional assistant for UC San Diego’s “Principle of Data Science,” or DSC10. This was an introductory course to data science. This was by far one of the most rewarding jobs I have had, as I was not only able to rekindle my initial interest and love of data science, but I was also able to contribute to UC San Diego’s data science community. Qualcomm Institute

La Jolla, CA / 2018-2019 / Research Intern

Through UC San Diego’s Academic Internship Program (or AIP), in which students can participate in an internship whilst receiving school credit, I was able to intern at the Qualcomm Institute. I was blown away by the innovative research in computer science and technology happening in front of my eyes. I developed a VR experience that helped induce sleep, based on research about the topic. As this was my first non-analytics project, I first felt the exhilaration of building a working product from ground up.

San Diego Gas and Electric

San Diego, CA / 2018 - 2019 / Business Analyst Intern

By this time, I had finished my first year studying data science. I spent majority of my time fulfilling data requests and researching/compiling information about consumers, and very quickly began to brainstorm methods to speed up these processes. I developed two projects: one that created a database of businesses in the area and programmatically assigned them to NAICS codes, a manual task that we normally spent hours a week working on, and another that automated their third-party requests validation process.

Hong Kong Computational Twitter Bots

(Python / WIP)

Misinformation in this technological era has always been an area of interest for me. As a Chinese-American whose parents grew up in Hong Kong, the Hong Kong democracy movement was always a major point of discussion at the dinner table. Upon seeing news of Twitter banning hundreds of thousands of Chinese computational twitter bots directed at the Hong Kong protests, I immediately submitted a proposal to research this topic for HDSI’s Undergraduate Research Scholarship.

CAIDA IPv4 Allocation Visualization

(WIP)

This webpage was created to educate the general public about internet address allocation, address exahaustion, and IPv4. It is presented in the form of an animated sankey diagram with changing slides.

Disney Song Lyrics Visualization

(Python Vader / d3.js)

Using the vader package in Python, I conducted sentiment analysis on nearly every Disney song, and created a visualization dashboard using what I learned from the analysis.

Generative Card Game Using RNN

(React.js / Python Keras)

I compiled text rules from hundreds of existing card game rules sheets (found on the internet) and generated my own new set of rules using a RNN LSTM model trained on my data. After the creation of the rules, I created a working version of the card game using React.js.
